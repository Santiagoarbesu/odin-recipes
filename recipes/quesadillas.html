Quesadillas

Description

A quesadilla is a Mexican dish consisting of a tortilla that is filled primarily with cheese, and sometimes meats, spices, and other fillings, and then cooked on a griddle or stove. Traditionally, a corn tortilla is used, but it can also be made with a flour tortilla.

Ingredients

  • 10 corn tortillas
  • 2 Cups of shredded cheese
  • 1 oz of black olives
  • 2 Avocados
  • 2 tablespoons of hot pepper sauce

Steps

  1. Heat a large frying pan or griddle over a medium heat.
  2. Place one tortilla flat on the frying pan. Cook for 1 minute, then flip the tortilla over.
  3. Sprinkle a little more than 1/4 cup cheese on top of tortilla, followed by some olives, avocado, and hot pepper sauce.
  4. Place another tortilla on top to make a sandwich; cover with a lid. Cook for 1 minute, then flip the quesadilla.
  5. Cook until cheese has melted on the inside; transfer quesadilla to a plate. Repeat with remaining ingredients.
  6. Cut the quesadillas into triangles and serve.
